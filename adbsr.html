Super Resolution for Sustainable Development

Collaborators: Son Le, Sebastian Mueller
Advisor: Yohan Idawella (Asian Development Bank), Konrad Schindler(ETH Zurich)

About

High-resolution satellite imagery is essential for environmental monitoring, urban planning, and disaster management. However, the high costs of acquiring such data limit its accessibility, especially for applications requiring extensive time-series analysis. Governments and researchers often need months or years of data to track trends, compounding costs to unsustainable levels. To address these challenges, the Asian Development Bank (ADB) proposes to develop an open-source deep learning model to upscale freely available Sentinel-2 imagery to high-resolution equivalents, offering an affordable and scalable solution for Earth Observation (EO) applications. Prior works have benchmarked super-resolution approaches to generating visually appealing images however the usage of super-resolution images downstream is lightly explored. Due to the limited data availability, existing super-resolution methods have not been designed in the context of Asian cities and evaluated for use in a time-series analysis. Leveraging deep-learning models, this approach lowers the barriers to accessing high-resolution EO data while enabling critical time-series analysis across large areas.

Contribution: Large Scale Computing, Computer Vision, Deep Learning
